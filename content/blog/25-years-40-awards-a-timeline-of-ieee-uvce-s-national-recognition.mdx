# 25 Years, 40+ Awards: A Timeline of IEEE UVCE’s National Recognition

Published on myuvce.in | Celebrating the Silver Jubilee of IEEE UVCE

In the quietest part of the bustling K.R. Circle, under the watchful eyes of Sri M. Visvesvaraya, lies a legacy that transcends mere academic curriculum. As we wind up 2025, we are not just marking another year; we are celebrating a monumental quarter-century. We dedicate this post to the **Silver Jubilee of IEEE UVCE**, a student branch that began as a modest initiative in the year 2000 and has since evolved into one of the most enduring and awarded technical communities in the country.

To sustain a volunteer-driven organization for 25 years is no easy feat. It requires passion that survives graduation, mentorship that spans decades, and a culture of "Selfless Action" that is passed down like a torch from one batch to the next. What started after two previous attempts in the late 70s and early 90s finally took root at the turn of the millennium. Today, we look back at a timeline studded with over **40 prestigious awards**, international recognition, and thousands of transformed lives.

## The Genesis: "Rejig to Reform"

The road to establishing a stable IEEE student branch at UVCE was paved with perseverance. Dr. Venugopal K R, the branch patron and former Vice Chancellor of Bangalore University, recalls that the success story began only after earlier attempts in 1977-78 and 1991-92 could not be sustained. It was the third determined attempt in **2000** that finally succeeded. Under the guidance of Dr. Venugopal and the relentless energy of Dr. P Deepa Shenoy, the branch was revitalized in 2001.

The vision was simple yet ambitious: to create a platform for UVCE students that supports learning, encourages collaboration, and fosters growth beyond the syllabus. In 2001, the branch launched **IMPETUS**, its flagship technical fest. By 2002, the membership had grown by 80%, and the branch was already making waves, securing its first major recognition as the "Best Student Chapter" for South India within just two years of its revival.

## A Legacy of Excellence

"Selfless Action is a True Volunteer's Real Identity"

This motto has driven the branch to accumulate over 40 awards from the IEEE Bangalore Section, IEEE India Council, and IEEE Region 10 (Asia Pacific).

## Timeline of Major Milestones

2001

### The Beginning

IMPETUS, the annual national-level technical fest, was organized for the first time, "Lighting the Lamp" for decades of technical excellence.

2005

### Global Representation

Nachiket Karajagi, the then Student General Secretary, represented the IEEE Bangalore Section at the Region 10 Student Congress in Hong Kong. This year also saw the launch of **KAGADA**, the National Level Paper Presentation Contest.

2007

### Research & Documentation

The Research wing, 'Avishkar', was started with the concept "Think, Innovate, Explore". The branch also released a souvenir titled "BIMBA", mirroring its achievements.

2008-09

### Diversity & Inclusion

The **Women in Engineering (WIE)** affinity group was inaugurated to promote women's participation in technology. Simultaneously, the Robotics Club and nCode (Programmers Club) were established.

2016

### International Acclaim

IEEE UVCE won the **Silver Award** in the IEEE Global Student Branch Website Contest. The Computer Society chapter was officially instituted, paving the way for specialized technical growth.

2019

### Gold Standard

KAGADA received the prestigious **Darrel Chong Student Activity Award (Gold)**, a global recognition for high-quality student activities.

2025

### Silver Jubilee

IEEE UVCE celebrates 25 years of uninterrupted excellence, launching the BRIDGE initiative to connect research, innovation, and entrepreneurship.

## More Than Just Awards: The "School of Life"

While the certificates and trophies line the shelves, the true reward of IEEE UVCE lies in the stories of its alumni. For over two decades, this organization has served as a finishing school for thousands of engineers. It is here that students who entered as hesitant freshers transformed into confident leaders capable of organizing national-level festivals.

> "As a 2022 graduate, I've seen myself and several of my friends go from 'I can't do this' to 'I didn't know I could do this'. There is an undeniable sense of community in the air—the seniors staying back to guide juniors, the alumni who come back as mentors, the volunteers coming in early to set up the stage."

The branch has produced an impressive roster of alumni who are now global leaders. From **Sachin Katti** (CTO at Intel, now leading infrastructure at OpenAI) to **Rahul Patil** (CTO of Anthropic) and **Amar Subramanya** (VP of AI at Apple), the roots of UVCE's engineering prowess run deep. The skills honed in the IEEE room—managing finances, dealing with ambiguity, pitching to sponsors, and leading teams—are the very skills that these alumni use to shape the global technology landscape today.

### Innovation During Crisis

The resilience of IEEE UVCE was most visible during the global pandemic. While the world stood still in 2020 and 2021, the branch adapted quickly. They moved flagship events like IMPETUS and KAGADA online, ensuring that the spirit of inquiry didn't fade. This period also saw the release of "REWIND 2020", a memoir documenting the first two decades, and the winning of the "Outstanding Large Student Branch" award in 2021, proving that the branch thrives even under pressure.

## Looking Ahead: The Next 25 Years

As we celebrate the Silver Jubilee in 2025, the vision for the future is clear. The launch of the **BRIDGE** initiative (Building Research & Innovation, Driving Growth & Entrepreneurship) marks a new chapter. By combining the strengths of MARVEL (Innovation Hub) and the E-Cell, IEEE UVCE is positioning itself as a powerhouse for technopreneurial thinking.

The goal for the next quarter-century is to deepen industry connections, launch new technical societies, and continue the tradition of academic rigor. The establishment of Special Interest Groups (SIGs) in Art, Design, and Humanitarian Technology ensures that the branch remains holistic, producing engineers who are not just technically sound but also socially responsible.

From the early days of inception to today's nationally recognized platform, IEEE UVCE has been a known name in the community. As we step into the next era, we carry forward the spirit that makes UVCE what it is. **Here's to 25 years of excellence, 40+ awards, and a future that looks even brighter.**
