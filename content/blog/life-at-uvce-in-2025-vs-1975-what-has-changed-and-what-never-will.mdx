There has been a drastic change in UVCE when you compare it over a span of 50 years. It has certainly evolved. The infrastructure, teaching methodologies, exam patterns, code of conduct, and the library have all transformed.

## Infrastructure and Development

The infrastructure has certainly bettered, though it is still an ongoing upgrade. There still needs to be improvements in the laboratories currently, specifically the Physics lab and that PPS lab above the library. 

Infrastructure-wise, the government has recently supplied significant funds to bring the campus into the modern era.

## Administration & Environment

The famous [“Nale Banni” (Come Tomorrow) culture of the 1975s](/2026/01/the-nale-banni-culture-history.html) is, thankfully, a myth now. The office staff are friendly, respond to queries, and actively try to solve student issues. There is no longer a scene of a sleepy clerk sitting outside the office.

UVCE has shed its stereotype of a typical, slow-moving government college and elevated itself to the level of a disciplined, distinct, and premier institution. Because of the amazing environment, flexible schedule, and stellar placement statistics, **[it remains a top choice for KCET toppers as of 2025](/2026/01/student-testimonials-why-uvce-top-choice-cet.html)**.

## The Canteen Chronicles

The canteen culture has seen its ups and downs. Back then, the old UVCE canteen was famous for its “Bisi Bele Bath” and the affectionately (and questionably) named [“Masala Cockroach Dosa”](/2026/01/uvce-canteen-story-decades-history.html). 

While that specific joint is gone, today we have a Nandini Canteen at the top of the RG, fulfilling the legacy of campus eateries. The stories around the canteen will never change. It was, is, and will continue to be the site of refreshment and joy. The spirit of friendships, senior-junior relationships, and the raw enthusiasm of engineering are aspects where change will never be seen. (Though, of course, ragging is strictly a thing of the past).

## Discipline & Innovation

A strictness and discipline have been inducted in recent years. The days of exam-mania gang roaming are over; teachers can now walk the campus without fear, commanding full esteem.

More importantly, student-driven innovation has taken over the campus culture.
